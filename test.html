Amazing Science Facts

Physics

Time Slows Near Black Holes

Due to extreme gravitational forces, time moves slower near black holes. An hour near a black hole could be equivalent to years on Earth, a phenomenon predicted by Einstein's theory of relativity.

Quantum Physics 1915
Biology

Human DNA in Every Cell

If you unraveled all the DNA molecules in your body and placed them end to end, it would stretch from Earth to Pluto and back — about 10 billion miles! Each cell contains about 2 meters of DNA.

Genetics 1953
Astronomy

Galactic Collision Coming

Our Milky Way galaxy is on a collision course with the Andromeda galaxy. But don't worry — this cosmic event won't happen for about 4 billion years, and stars are so far apart that direct collisions are unlikely.

Astrophysics 2012
Chemistry

Diamonds from Space

Scientists have discovered planets that may be composed largely of diamond. One such planet, 55 Cancri e, is twice Earth's size and may have a surface of graphite and diamond rather than water and granite.

Mineralogy 2011
Neuroscience

Brain's Storage Capacity

The human brain's memory capacity is estimated to be around 2.5 petabytes (or a million gigabytes). For comparison, this is about the same amount needed to store 3 million hours of TV shows.

Cognitive Science 2016
Quantum Physics

Quantum Entanglement

Quantum particles can become "entangled," meaning the state of one instantly influences another, no matter how far apart they are. Einstein called this "spooky action at a distance," and it's been experimentally verified.

Quantum Mechanics 1935

Historic Scientific Discoveries

1543

Heliocentric Model

Nicolaus Copernicus published his theory that the Earth revolves around the Sun, revolutionizing our understanding of the solar system and challenging the geocentric model that had dominated for centuries.

1687

Laws of Motion

Isaac Newton published "Philosophiæ Naturalis Principia Mathematica," formulating the laws of motion and universal gravitation, laying the foundation for classical mechanics.

1859

Theory of Evolution

Charles Darwin published "On the Origin of Species," presenting compelling evidence for evolution by natural selection, fundamentally changing our understanding of life's diversity.

1905

Theory of Relativity

Albert Einstein published his special theory of relativity, introducing the famous equation E=mc² and revolutionizing our concepts of space, time, and energy.

1953

DNA Structure

James Watson and Francis Crick discovered the double helix structure of DNA, the molecule that carries genetic information in all living organisms, opening the door to modern genetics.

2012

Higgs Boson

Scientists at CERN confirmed the discovery of the Higgs boson, the particle that gives other particles mass, completing the Standard Model of particle physics.
